Deganwy, UK

The name Deganwy has been interpreted in modern times as Din-Gonwy, which would mean "Fort on the River Conwy". The town was once the home of Deganwy Castle, now in ruins, which was the capital of the Kingdom of Gwynedd, eventually being demolished by Llywelyn ap Gruffydd in 1263.
